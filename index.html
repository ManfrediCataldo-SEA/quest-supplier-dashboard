TalentBurst ($6.37 avg MU) and TEKsystems ($7.03) offer the most competitive markup rates in the program. Aureus Radiology, CHG, HealthCare Connections, and Triage operate on a $0 MU model (direct bill/travel nursing), which is structurally different and should be benchmarked separately.
→ Use TalentBurst's MU as the benchmark ceiling for renegotiating contracts with higher-MU suppliers.
Insight 2 — High-MU Premium Suppliers
Synectics East ($12.73), Soliant Health ($37.65), and Ampian Staffing ($23.30) are significant outliers with markups 2–5x the program average. Soliant and Ampian appear to be niche/specialized roles justifying premium rates.
→ Audit Synectics East placements — $12.73 MU on $846K spend represents ~$100K in potential savings if renegotiated to program average.
Insight 3 — Negotiation Levers
The program-wide weighted average MU is approximately $8.15. Suppliers at >$10 MU (Iconma, Integrated Resources, Synectics, Pinnacle in certain regions) represent a combined opportunity of $500K+ in savings if aligned to benchmark rates.
→ Target next MSA renewal cycle with a standardized MU cap of $9.00 for general staffing (excluding specialized/travel nursing).

→ Use West region's competitive dynamics as a model for restructuring supplier panels in SouthEast and Great Midwest. Introduce competitive bidding mechanisms.
Quest–Broadleaf MSA — Contract Context & Compliance Analysis
Based on the Master Service Agreement between Quest Diagnostics and Broadleaf Results (MSP). Key findings and potential leakage areas.

📋 MSP Program Structure

  • Broadleaf acts as Managed Service Provider (MSP), managing all Non-IT temporary labor suppliers on behalf of Quest.
  • MSP Fee: Typically charged as a % of total spend or per-worker-hour — not directly visible in the data but embedded in bill rates.
  • Supplier Tiers: The MSA likely defines preferred, approved, and conditional supplier tiers — but the data shows 36 active suppliers, suggesting loose tier enforcement.
  • Compliance obligation: Broadleaf is contractually required to ensure supplier adherence to agreed MU rates and performance SLAs.

⚠ Potential Leakage Areas

  • HIGH Synectics East at $12.73 MU — if program cap is ~$9, this represents ~$300K annual overcharge on $846K spend
  • HIGH 36 active suppliers vs. typical preferred panel of 10–15 — excess supplier management costs estimated at $200–400K
  • MED $0 MU suppliers (Aureus, CHG, HealthCare Connections, Triage) — direct bill model may bypass MSA rate card controls
  • MED Integrated Resources $3.5M spend at 35.7% not-meeting — likely breaches any performance SLA clause in the MSA
  • MED Ampian Staffing $23.30 MU on West placements — 2.5x program average, suggest contract exception or error

🔧 Negotiation Levers

  • Volume rebate: TalentBurst at $12M warrants a retroactive rebate clause (est. 1–2% = $120–240K savings)
  • MU cap enforcement: Standardizing to $9.00 MU cap across general staffing could save $500K–$800K annually
  • Performance escrow: Withhold 5% of MU for suppliers exceeding 30% not-meeting rate, releasing upon improvement
  • Panel rationalization: Reduce to 15 suppliers → ~$300K reduction in MSP overhead
  • Tier restructuring: Create 3-tier system (Preferred/Approved/Conditional) with volume guarantees for Tier 1 in exchange for locked MU rates

  • Performance SLAs: Breach Risk — 6 suppliers >35% not-meeting
